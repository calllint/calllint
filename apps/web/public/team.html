CallLint Team · design-partner beta

Keep local scanning free. Centralize the rest.

The CallLint CLI is free and stays free — scanning, verdicts, the Trust Gateway, and Continuous Guard all run locally and offline. What teams ask for next is not a bigger scanner: it's a shared place for policy, approvals, receipts, and drift evidence across repos and people. That is what CallLint Team is.

We are opening a small design-partner beta to build it with a handful of teams. It is not on sale yet — this page is where you tell us it should exist.

Free vs. Team — an explicit boundary

We will not move anything you have today behind a paywall. The line is simple: local, single-developer work is free forever; Team adds the shared, cross-repo, multi-person layer that only makes sense for an organization.

Free — local CLI, forever

  • Scan MCP servers and agent tools — SAFE / REVIEW / BLOCK / UNKNOWN with evidence.
  • Trust Gateway: prepare → approve → apply → verify, with reversible install plans.
  • Continuous Guard on authority change; local decision receipts (sign/verify).
  • CI gate and the GitHub Action, run from your own repo.

Team — the shared layer

  • One org policy, versioned, that every repo's scan and CI pulls.
  • Shared approvals and a private baseline — one place to say "this is allowed here."
  • Durable decision receipts and drift evidence across repos, kept as an audit trail.
  • Cross-repo inventory of what your agents can reach, and who approved it.
Team never changes a verdict. The engine stays deterministic and local; the shared layer stores policy, approvals, and evidence — it does not decide security for you.

Price-range test

CallLint Team Design Partner — $99 / org / month. Design partners help us set final pricing and get founder terms. Nothing is charged during the beta; this is a willingness signal, not a checkout.

What being a design partner means

  • A short intake, then a 30-minute interview about how your team handles agent-tool and MCP risk today.
  • Early access to the shared layer as we build it, and direct influence on what ships first.
  • No obligation to buy. If the willingness is not there, we would rather learn that now.
